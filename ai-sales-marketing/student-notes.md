### Prompt Engineering for Marketing

**CRAFT Framework:**
- **C**ontext - What's the situation?
- **R**ole - Who should AI be?
- **A**ction - What should it do?
- **F**ormat - How should output look?
- **T**one - What style/feeling?

**Weak Prompt:**
```
Write a marketing email.
```

**Strong Prompt:**
```
Role: You are a B2B email marketing specialist.

Context: Our SaaS product helps [industry] companies reduce [pain point] by 40%.

Task: Write a follow-up email to prospects who attended our webinar but haven't booked a demo.

Format: Subject line + 150-word email. Include one customer proof point and one clear CTA.

Tone: Helpful, not pushy. Professional but conversational.
```

---

### Marketing Prompt Templates

**Email Campaign:**
```
Write a [type] email for [audience].
Goal: [action]
Key benefit: [value prop]
Include: Subject line, preview text, body (under 200 words), CTA button text.
```

**Social Media:**
```
Create 5 [platform] posts about [topic].
Audience: [persona]
Brand voice: [tone]
Include: Hook, value, hashtags, CTA.
```

**Blog Post:**
```
Write a blog post: "[title]"
Target keyword: [SEO keyword]
Reader level: [beginner/expert]
Include: H2 sections, examples, actionable tips, meta description.
```

## Day 2: Analytics, Scoring & Implementation

### AI Lead Scoring

**What It Is:** Machine learning predicts which leads are most likely to convert based on historical data patterns.

**Traditional vs. AI Scoring:**

| Traditional | AI-Powered |
|-------------|------------|
| Manual point assignment | Learns from closed-won patterns |
| Based on assumptions | Considers 100+ signals |
| Static rules | Updates in real-time |
| Limited factors | Explains score changes |

**Key Insight:** AI scoring typically improves lead-to-opportunity conversion by 30-50%.

---

### Signals AI Uses for Lead Scoring

| Category | Signals |
|----------|---------|
| **Demographic** | Job title, seniority, department, company size, industry, location |
| **Firmographic** | Revenue, growth rate, tech stack, funding stage, hiring signals |
| **Behavioral** | Page visits, content downloads, email engagement, form submissions, chat interactions |
| **Temporal** | Recency of activity, frequency patterns, time to conversion, engagement velocity |

**Pattern Recognition Example:**
"Leads who visit the integrations page, then pricing, within 3 days of signing up for a webinar convert 4x more often."

### The Crawl-Walk-Run Approach

**Crawl (Week 1-2):**
- Pick ONE use case
- Use free AI tools (ChatGPT)
- Document time savings
- Share wins with team

**Walk (Month 1-2):**
- Expand to 3-5 use cases
- Create prompt templates
- Train team members
- Evaluate paid tools

**Run (Month 3+):**
- Integrate with CRM/MAP
- Implement lead scoring
- Build AI workflows
- Measure ROI

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| Publish AI content without review | Always have human review and edit |
| Put sensitive data in AI tools | Use enterprise tools or anonymize data |
| Expect perfection on first try | Iterate on prompts and refine outputs |
| Try to automate everything at once | Start small, measure results, expand |
| Ignore your brand voice | Include brand guidelines in prompts |
| Skip measurement | Track time saved and business outcomes |

---

### Ethical AI in Marketing

**Privacy:**
- Don't feed PII to public AI
- Respect data consent
- Use enterprise tools for sensitive data
- Anonymize when possible

**Transparency:**
- Disclose AI use when appropriate
- Don't fake human interaction
- Be honest about AI limitations
- Label AI-generated images

**Fairness:**
- Watch for bias in targeting
- Ensure inclusive content
- Test across demographics
- Don't manipulate or deceive

**Key Insight:** 83% of consumers want to know when they're interacting with AI.
