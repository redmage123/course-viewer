Pre-Session Preparation

Before the Session (24 hours)

  • Test internet connection at venue
  • Verify access to demo accounts (NotebookLM, ChatGPT)
  • Prepare sample documents (see below)
  • Test screen sharing setup
  • Print student notes as backup
  • Prepare fallback slides in case demos fail

Sample Documents to Prepare

Create or use these sample documents for the live demo:

Option A: HR Policy Demo

  • Vacation/PTO Policy (1-2 pages)
  • Expense Reimbursement Guide
  • Remote Work Policy
  • Benefits Summary

Option B: Product Manual Demo

  • Product FAQ document
  • Troubleshooting guide
  • Setup instructions
  • Feature descriptions
Tip: Use real-looking but fictional content to avoid privacy concerns. Or use publicly available documents like government guides, open-source project documentation, etc.

Demo 1: NotebookLM 10-12 minutes

Setup (Before Session)

  • Go to notebooklm.google.com
  • Create a new notebook called "HR Policy Assistant"
  • Upload 3-4 sample HR documents
  • Have it ready but minimized

Live Demo Script

"Now I'm going to show you how easy it is to build a knowledge base using a free tool from Google called NotebookLM. This is great for research, document analysis, and creating AI assistants grounded in your own content."
Open NotebookLM and show the pre-created notebook with documents already uploaded.
"I've already uploaded some sample HR policy documents here. Let me show you what we can do with them."
Type a question: "How many vacation days do new employees get?"
"Notice how the AI gives us an answer, but more importantly, look at these citations on the right side. Each numbered reference links back to the exact section in our documents. This is called 'grounding' - the AI only answers from what's in our knowledge base."
Click on a citation to show it highlights the source text.
"Let me try another question that tests the boundaries..."
Ask: "What is the salary range for senior engineers?"
"See? It tells us this information isn't in the documents. That's exactly what we want - it won't make things up!"

Bonus Feature (if time permits)

"NotebookLM has a cool feature - it can generate an audio summary of your documents, like a podcast. Let me show you..."
Click "Generate Audio Overview" and play a short clip (or show a pre-generated one).

Questions to Ask During Demo

  • "How many vacation days do new employees get?"
  • "What's the process for submitting expenses?"
  • "Can I work from home on Fridays?"
  • "What are the health insurance options?"
  • "What's the salary range for engineers?" (should fail - not in docs)

Demo 2: Custom GPT 10-12 minutes

Setup (Before Session)

  • Have ChatGPT Plus account ready ($20/mo required)
  • Pre-create a Custom GPT with documents uploaded
  • OR be prepared to create one live (takes ~5 minutes)

Live Demo Script - Creating a Custom GPT

"Now let's look at another approach - Custom GPTs in ChatGPT. This is available with a ChatGPT Plus subscription and lets you create specialized assistants."
Go to ChatGPT → Explore GPTs → Create (top right)
"You can either use the conversation mode or the Configure tab. Let's use Configure for more control."
Click "Configure" tab and fill in: - Name: "HR Policy Helper" - Description: "Answers questions about company policies"
"Here's where the magic happens - the Instructions field. This is your system prompt that tells the GPT how to behave."
Paste the system prompt from the slides (or type it).
You are an HR Policy Assistant for our company. RULES: 1. ONLY answer from the uploaded documents 2. ALWAYS cite which document the answer came from 3. If the answer isn't in the documents, say: "I couldn't find that in our policy documents. Please contact HR at hr@company.com" 4. Be friendly and professional FORMAT: - Give a direct answer first - Quote the relevant policy section - End with the source document name
Scroll down to "Knowledge" and upload documents.
"Now we upload our documents. These become the knowledge base that the GPT will search when answering questions."
Test the GPT with sample questions.
Common Issues:
  • Documents not uploading: Max 20 files, 512MB total. Try smaller files.
  • Wrong answers: The system prompt needs to be more explicit about only using uploaded docs.
  • Slow responses: Normal for Custom GPTs. Wait 5-10 seconds.

Demo 3: Side-by-Side Comparison 5 minutes

Comparing the Approaches

"Let's quickly compare what we just saw. Both tools accomplish the same goal but have different strengths."
Show this comparison (or refer to slides):
Feature NotebookLM Custom GPT
Cost Free $20/month
Citations Excellent (highlights) Good (text references)
Sharing Link sharing Public or link
Audio summaries Yes No
Customization Limited High (system prompts)
Best for Research, personal use Team tools, production

Handling Questions & Troubleshooting

Anticipated Questions

  • "Is my data safe?" - Both tools have enterprise options. Free tiers may use data for training. Check vendor policies.
  • "Can I use this with our internal systems?" - Custom GPTs can use APIs. Enterprise tools like Microsoft Copilot integrate with M365.
  • "What about PDFs with images/tables?" - Both handle text well. Complex layouts may need preprocessing.
  • "How do I prevent hallucinations?" - Strong system prompts, require citations, test edge cases.
  • "Can multiple people use the same copilot?" - Yes, both support sharing. Enterprise needs vary.

Fallback Plans

If demos fail...

  • Internet down: Switch to slides with screenshots of the demo flow
  • Account issues: Have a recorded video backup
  • Slow responses: Pre-cache some responses by asking questions before the session
  • Unexpected answers: Use it as a teaching moment about prompt engineering
