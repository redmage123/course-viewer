1. Introduction: The Document Challenge

Business documents like proposals and reports consume significant time and resources. The goal isn't just speed - it's maintaining quality while accelerating production.

Key Statistics:
  • Business proposals typically take 4-20 hours to create
  • Reports require 2-8 hours on average
  • Up to 80% of content is often repetitive across documents
  • Version control and consistency are major challenges

Document Types Covered

  • Business Proposals (RFPs, bids, pitches)
  • Reports (status, analysis, research)
  • Executive Summaries
  • Case Studies
  • SOWs & Contracts
  • Formal Communications

2. The WRITE Framework

A structured approach to AI-assisted document creation that ensures comprehensive, high-quality output.

W What & Why

Define the document's purpose and context:

  • Document Type: What are you creating?
  • Purpose: What should it achieve?
  • Audience: Who will read it? What do they need?
  • Outcome: What action should the reader take?

R Requirements

Specify structural and compliance requirements:

Structural

  • Required sections/headings
  • Page/word count limits
  • Format specifications
  • Mandatory inclusions

Compliance

  • Legal disclaimers
  • Regulatory language
  • Brand guidelines
  • Approval workflows

I Information

Provide context and data to the AI:

  • Client/audience background and challenges
  • Previous interactions or history
  • Your product/service details
  • Key differentiators and case studies
  • Pricing structure (if applicable)
Pro Tip: The more specific context you provide, the more tailored and accurate the output will be.

T Tone & Template

Define the style and format:

Tone Type Description Best For
Professional Formal, corporate Enterprise clients
Consultative Advisory, expert Services proposals
Persuasive Compelling, urgent Sales documents
Technical Detailed, precise Technical audiences
Accessible Clear, jargon-free Non-technical readers

E Examples

Show the AI what good looks like:

  • Excerpts from winning proposals
  • Approved language samples
  • Style guide references
  • Both good and bad examples (what to avoid)

3. Guardrails: Ensuring Quality & Compliance

Guardrails are the safety mechanisms that ensure AI-generated content meets your standards.

Quality Guardrails

Built into Prompts

  • Specify required sections
  • Define minimum detail level
  • Request fact-checking flags
  • Ask for source citations

Post-Generation Checks

  • Completeness review
  • Accuracy verification
  • Readability scoring
  • Peer review step

Compliance Guardrails

Area Guardrail Approach
Legal Disclaimers Pre-approved text blocks included verbatim
Data Privacy (GDPR) Checklist of required privacy statements
Industry Regulations Sector-specific language requirements
Contract Terms Standard T&Cs with approval triggers
Brand Guidelines Approved terminology and tone standards
Critical Rule: Never let AI generate or modify legal, contractual, or compliance language without expert review.

Security Guardrails

Never Share with AI

  • Customer personal data
  • Financial details
  • Passwords/credentials
  • Confidential strategy
  • Trade secrets

Safe to Share

  • Public company info
  • General requirements
  • Anonymized examples
  • Generic templates
  • Published case studies

Proposal Prompt Template

Executive Summary (max 200 words) 2. Understanding of Needs 3. Proposed Solution 4. Implementation Approach 5. Timeline & Milestones 6. Investment & ROI 7. Why Choose Us 8. Next Steps # STYLE - Professional but approachable tone - Active voice - Quantify benefits where possible - Include call-to-action in each section

Report Prompt Template

Executive Summary (key findings in 3 bullets) 2. Background/Context 3. Methodology (if applicable) 4. Key Findings (with supporting data) 5. Analysis & Implications 6. Recommendations 7. Appendix (detailed data) # GUIDELINES - Lead with insights, not data - Use charts/tables where helpful - Highlight risks and opportunities - Include confidence levels for projections - Flag items needing verification with [VERIFY]

Refinement Prompts

Goal Prompt Example
Expand "Expand section 3 with more detail on implementation"
Shorten "Shorten the executive summary to 150 words"
Strengthen "Add specific examples to support the key claims"
Simplify "Remove jargon and simplify for non-technical readers"
Review "Identify 5 ways to make this more compelling"

5. Best Practices Summary

Document Creation Workflow

  1. Prepare: Gather all WRITE framework inputs before starting
  2. Generate Outline: Get structure first, then sections
  3. Build Sections: Generate one section at a time for better control
  4. Apply Guardrails: Run quality and compliance checks
  5. Human Review: Always verify facts, numbers, and commitments
  6. Final Polish: Use AI for consistency check and final refinement

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Generating entire documents in one prompt (too much for quality control)
  • Skipping the context/information step (generic output)
  • Not providing examples (inconsistent style)
  • Trusting AI output without verification (factual errors)
  • Sharing sensitive data with public AI tools
  • Forgetting compliance requirements

7. Quick Reference: Guardrails Checklist

Quality

  • All required sections complete
  • No placeholder text remaining
  • Claims supported by evidence
  • Numbers/dates marked for verification
  • Tone consistent throughout

Compliance

  • Required disclaimers included
  • Regulatory language correct
  • No unauthorized commitments
  • Pricing approved
  • Terms reviewed by legal (if needed)

Consistency

  • Client name correct throughout
  • Product names match brand guidelines
  • Voice matches brand personality
  • Formatting follows standards

Security

  • No sensitive data exposed to AI
  • Confidential info anonymized
  • Document marked with appropriate classification
