The WRITE Framework

A structured approach to AI-assisted document creation

WRITE
W What & Why
R Requirements
I Information
T Tone & Template
E Examples
9

W - What & Why

1
Document Type: What are you creating? (proposal, report, summary)
2
Purpose: What should this document achieve?
3
Audience: Who will read this? What do they need?
4
Outcome: What action should the reader take?
Example:
"I need to write a business proposal for a mid-sized healthcare company to convince them to implement our data analytics platform. The primary reader is the CTO who needs to approve the budget."
10

R - Requirements

Structural Requirements

  • Required sections/headings
  • Page/word count limits
  • Format specifications
  • Mandatory inclusions

Compliance Requirements

  • Legal disclaimers
  • Regulatory language
  • Brand guidelines
  • Approval workflows
Example:
"The proposal must include: Executive Summary, Problem Statement, Proposed Solution, Implementation Timeline, Pricing, and Terms. Maximum 10 pages. Must include GDPR compliance statement and standard liability disclaimer."
11

I - Information

Provide the AI with relevant context and data

Client/Audience Info

  • Company background
  • Current challenges
  • Previous interactions
  • Decision criteria

Your Offering Info

  • Product/service details
  • Key differentiators
  • Relevant case studies
  • Pricing structure
Pro Tip: The more specific context you provide, the more tailored and accurate the output will be.
12

T - Tone & Template

Tone Descriptors

  • Professional - formal, corporate
  • Consultative - advisory, expert
  • Persuasive - compelling, urgent
  • Technical - detailed, precise
  • Accessible - clear, jargon-free

Template Elements

  • Document structure
  • Section order
  • Standard language blocks
  • Formatting conventions
  • Visual elements
Example:
"Use a professional but approachable tone. Write in active voice. Keep sentences concise (max 25 words). Use bullet points for lists. Follow our standard proposal template with blue headers and grey body text."
13

E - Examples

Show the AI what good looks like

What to Include

  • Excerpts from winning proposals
  • Approved language samples
  • Style guide references
  • Successful document sections

How to Use

  • "Write in this style: [example]"
  • "Match this tone: [example]"
  • "Structure like this: [example]"
  • "Avoid this approach: [example]"
Best Practice: Build a library of approved examples for different document types that you can quickly reference.

Quality Guardrails

Built into Prompts

  • Specify required sections
  • Define minimum detail level
  • Request fact-checking flags
  • Ask for source citations
  • Include quality criteria

Post-Generation Checks

  • Completeness review
  • Accuracy verification
  • Readability scoring
  • Grammar/style check
  • Peer review step
Quality Guardrail Prompt:
"Before finalizing, verify that: (1) All required sections are complete, (2) No placeholder text remains, (3) All claims have supporting evidence, (4) Numbers and dates are marked for verification, (5) Tone is consistent throughout."
16

Compliance Guardrails

Compliance Area Guardrail Approach
Legal Disclaimers Pre-approved text blocks that must be included verbatim
Data Privacy (GDPR) Checklist of required privacy statements; sensitive data handling
Industry Regulations Sector-specific language requirements; prohibited claims
Contract Terms Standard T&Cs that cannot be modified; approval triggers
Brand Guidelines Approved terminology; prohibited phrases; tone standards
Critical: Never let AI generate or modify legal, contractual, or compliance language without expert review.

  • Visual standards

Messaging

  • Key differentiators
  • Approved claims
  • Competitive positioning
Pro Tip: Create a "style guide prompt" that you prepend to all document generation requests to ensure consistency.

Security Guardrails

Never Share with AI

  • Customer personal data
  • Financial details
  • Passwords/credentials
  • Confidential strategy
  • Trade secrets
  • Unreleased product info

Safe to Share

  • Public company info
  • General requirements
  • Anonymized examples
  • Generic templates
  • Published case studies
  • Standard procedures
Security Rule: When in doubt, anonymize or generalize. Replace specific names, numbers, and details with placeholders that you fill in manually later.

The Proposal Prompt Template

# ROLE You are an expert business proposal writer with 15 years of experience in [INDUSTRY]. # TASK Write a compelling proposal for [CLIENT NAME] to [OBJECTIVE]. # CONTEXT - Client: [Company description, size, industry] - Challenge: [Their specific problem/need] - Our Solution: [What we're proposing] - Budget Range: [If known] - Timeline: [Expected delivery] # REQUIREMENTS Include these sections: 1. Executive Summary (max 200 words) 2. Understanding of Needs 3. Proposed Solution 4. Implementation Approach 5. Timeline & Milestones 6. Investment & ROI 7. Why Choose Us 8. Next Steps # STYLE - Professional but approachable tone - Active voice - Quantify benefits where possible - Include call-to-action in each section
22

The Report Prompt Template

# ROLE You are a senior analyst preparing a [TYPE] report for [AUDIENCE LEVEL] readers. # TASK Create a comprehensive report on [TOPIC] covering [TIME PERIOD]. # DATA & INPUTS [Paste relevant data, metrics, observations, or findings here] # STRUCTURE 1. Executive Summary (key findings in 3 bullets) 2. Background/Context 3. Methodology (if applicable) 4. Key Findings (with supporting data) 5. Analysis & Implications 6. Recommendations 7. Appendix (detailed data) # GUIDELINES - Lead with insights, not data - Use charts/tables where helpful (describe what to visualize) - Highlight risks and opportunities - Include confidence levels for projections - Flag items needing verification with [VERIFY]
23

Iterative Refinement Prompts

Expand & Enrich

  • "Expand section 3 with more detail on implementation"
  • "Add specific examples to support the key claims"
  • "Include industry statistics to strengthen the argument"

Refine & Tighten

  • "Shorten the executive summary to 150 words"
  • "Make the tone more confident and direct"
  • "Remove jargon and simplify for non-technical readers"
Power Move: After generating a draft, ask AI to "Review this document and identify 5 ways to make it more compelling and persuasive."
24

Section-by-Section Generation

For complex documents, build piece by piece

1
Outline First: "Create a detailed outline for a proposal about [X]"
2
Generate Sections: "Now write section 2: Understanding of Needs"
3
Review & Refine: "Make this section more specific to healthcare"
4
Assemble & Polish: "Review all sections for consistency and flow"
Why This Works: Keeps context focused, allows for targeted refinement, and maintains quality across longer documents.
