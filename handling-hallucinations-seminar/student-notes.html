Handling Hallucinations: Fact-Checking AI

ITAG Skillnet AI Advantage | Student Notes

What is an AI Hallucination?

An AI hallucination occurs when a language model generates content that is:

  • Factually incorrect - States something false as if it were true
  • Fabricated - Invents sources, quotes, statistics, or data that don't exist
  • Confidently wrong - Presents errors with high certainty
Key Insight: AI doesn't "know" it's wrong. LLMs generate plausible-sounding text based on patterns, not understanding or fact-checking.

Why Do Hallucinations Happen?

  • Pattern matching - LLMs predict what comes next based on patterns, not knowledge retrieval
  • Optimization for plausibility - Models are trained to sound coherent, not to be accurate
  • No fact database - LLMs can't verify claims against a source of truth
  • Training data limitations - Incomplete or outdated information in training data

Types of Hallucinations

Type Description Example
Intrinsic Contradicts the source/input provided Input says "Tuesday" but AI outputs "Wednesday"
Extrinsic Adds unverifiable information Summary includes facts not in the original document
Fabricated Citations Invents sources that don't exist References a fake journal article or book
Invented Statistics Creates precise numbers without basis "73.4% of companies report..."

The VERIFY Framework

V
Validate Sources - Check if cited sources actually exist. Search for papers, verify authors, check URLs.
E
Examine Specifics - Scrutinize precise numbers, dates, and names. Overly specific statistics are red flags.
R
Research Claims - Cross-reference with 2+ authoritative sources before using important claims.
I
Identify Uncertainty - Look for hedging language. AI confidence ≠ accuracy.
F
Flag Suspicious Content - Mark claims with [VERIFY] for later checking.
Y
Yield to Expertise - Consult domain experts for critical decisions.

High-Risk Domains

Critical Risk Areas:
  • Medical advice and health information
  • Legal citations and case law
  • Financial data and investment advice
  • Safety procedures and regulations
High Risk Areas:
  • Historical facts and dates
  • Scientific claims and research
  • Technical specifications
  • Current events (after training cutoff)

Prevention Strategies

1. Better Prompts

Add to your prompts: "Important guidelines: - Only include facts you are highly confident about - If you cite a source, it must be real and verifiable - Mark uncertain claims with [NEEDS VERIFICATION] - Say 'I don't know' rather than guessing - Don't invent statistics - use qualitative descriptions"

2. Ground with Context (RAG)

Provide verified source material in your prompt and instruct the AI to use ONLY that information:

"Based ONLY on the following data, summarize the key points: [Paste your verified data here] Do not add information not contained in the above."

3. Chain-of-Thought

Ask AI to show its reasoning so you can evaluate the logic:

"Think through this step by step, noting any assumptions you're making and areas of uncertainty."

4. Self-Critique

Ask the AI to review its own output for potential issues:

"Review your previous response and identify: 1. Claims that might be outdated 2. Statistics that need verification 3. Sources that should be confirmed 4. Statements that should be hedged"

Review Workflow

  1. Generate - Create content with anti-hallucination prompts
  2. Flag - Mark all verifiable claims (stats, quotes, sources)
  3. Verify - Check each flagged item against authoritative sources
  4. Revise - Correct errors, remove unverifiable claims
  5. Review - Expert check for domain-specific content
  6. Publish - Only after human sign-off

Quick Reference: Red Flags

  • Very precise percentages (73.4%, 42.6%)
  • Specific dates for vague events
  • Exact quotes from famous people
  • Detailed statistics without sources
  • "Studies show..." (which studies?)
  • Highly specific claims about recent events
  • Citations with full DOIs or URLs (check them!)
The Trust Equation:
AI Output + Human Verification = Trustworthy Content

AI provides speed, humans ensure accuracy.

Take-Home Exercise

Practice Assignment

  1. Generate - Ask AI to write a 200-word summary about a topic in your field
  2. Analyze - Apply the VERIFY framework to identify potential issues
  3. Verify - Fact-check at least 3 specific claims using authoritative sources
  4. Revise - Create a corrected version with proper sourcing
  5. Reflect - Document what you learned about AI limitations

Bonus: Try the same prompt with different AI models and compare hallucination patterns.
