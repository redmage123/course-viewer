L Landscape Assessment

Understand where AI fits in your organisation today

1
Audit existing processes: Map workflows and identify repetitive, data-heavy tasks
2
Assess data readiness: What data do you have? Is it clean, accessible, governed?
3
Evaluate infrastructure: Cloud vs. on-premise, compute capacity, security posture
4
Benchmark competitors: What are peers in your industry doing with AI?
Key Question: "Where do our people spend the most time on tasks a machine could assist with?"
Common Pitfall: Jumping to solutions before understanding the problem landscape. Always assess before you act.

A Alignment with Strategy

Ensure AI initiatives serve business objectives

1
Link to business goals: Every AI project should map to a strategic objective (revenue, efficiency, CX)
2
Secure executive sponsorship: AI needs top-down support and visible champions
3
Define success metrics: Agree on KPIs before starting the project
4
Prioritise ruthlessly: Not every process needs AI -- focus on highest impact

Strategic Alignment Checklist

  • Does this AI initiative address a top-3 business priority?
  • Is there an executive sponsor committed to this?
  • Can we measure success within 90 days?
  • Do we have the data and infrastructure?
  • Is the team willing and able to adopt it?

U Upskilling the Workforce

People are your most important AI asset

Tiered Training Approach

  • All Staff: AI literacy, responsible use, prompt basics
  • Power Users: Advanced prompting, workflow automation, tool selection
  • Technical Teams: Fine-tuning, RAG systems, deployment, MLOps
  • Leadership: Strategy, governance, risk management, ROI
Best Practice: Create internal "AI Champions" -- enthusiasts in each department who mentor colleagues and share use cases.
Do Not: Roll out tools without training. Untrained users produce poor results, lose trust, and abandon AI entirely.

N Navigate Change Management

Technology is easy; people are hard

Communicate

  • Be transparent about AI's role
  • Address job security fears directly
  • Share success stories early
  • Create feedback channels

Involve

  • Include end-users in pilot selection
  • Co-design workflows with teams
  • Let teams choose their first use case
  • Celebrate early adopters

Sustain

  • Track adoption metrics weekly
  • Address resistance with empathy
  • Iterate based on real feedback
  • Build AI into performance goals
Remember: Change management accounts for over 70% of AI project failures. Invest in people as much as technology.

H Harvest & Scale

Turn pilot successes into organisation-wide transformation

1
Document learnings: Capture what worked, what didn't, and why
2
Standardise processes: Create repeatable playbooks for AI deployment
3
Scale horizontally: Apply successful patterns to similar use cases across departments
4
Establish governance: Create AI policies, review boards, and ethical guidelines

Scaling Checklist

  • Pilot results documented and shared
  • ROI validated against initial metrics
  • Deployment playbook created
  • Governance framework in place
  • Training programme scaled
  • Budget allocated for next phase
Key: Scaling is not about doing more -- it is about doing what works, better and broader.

Common Rollout Pitfalls

Learn from others' mistakes

1. "Boiling the ocean"

Trying to transform everything at once. Start with one focused pilot.

2. Ignoring data quality

AI is only as good as the data it works with. Clean data first.

3. No executive sponsor

Without top-level backing, AI projects stall at the first obstacle.

4. Tool-first thinking

Buying tools before defining the problem. Always start with the use case.

5. Skipping change management

Great technology fails without people adoption. Invest in training and communication.

Antidote: Follow the LAUNCH framework step by step. Each phase builds on the previous one.
