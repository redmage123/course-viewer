## Evaluation Criteria for Tools

When evaluating AI tools for your organization, consider:

1. **Integration:** How well does it work with existing systems?
2. **Security:** Does it meet your data protection requirements?
3. **Scalability:** Can it grow with your needs?
4. **Support:** What training and support is available?
5. **Cost:** What's the total cost of ownership?
6. **Compliance:** Does it meet industry regulations?
7. **Customization:** Can it be tailored to your processes?

# Staying Current

## Weekly Habits

### Monday: Newsletter Catch-Up
Subscribe to these newsletters:
- **The Batch** (deeplearning.ai): Weekly AI news
- **Import AI** (Jack Clark): Research and policy
- **The Algorithm** (MIT Tech Review): AI news
- **AI Weekly** (Louie Peters): Curated AI content
- **Last Week in AI**: Research roundup

### Wednesday: Deep Read
Choose one article from your reading list and read it thoroughly.

### Friday: Practical Application
Try applying one new concept to your work context.

## Monthly Habits

### Week 1: Community Engagement
- Attend a meetup or webinar
- Participate in an online discussion
- Connect with a new AI practitioner

### Week 2: Tool Exploration
- Test a new AI tool
- Watch a tutorial on a platform you're curious about
- Document what you learned

### Week 3: Case Study Review
- Read 2-3 case studies in your industry
- Note what worked and what didn't
- Consider applications to your context

### Week 4: Reflection and Planning
- Review what you learned this month
- Update your learning priorities
- Plan next month's focus areas

## Quarterly Habits

### Skills Assessment
- Retake the self-assessment from this course
- Identify areas for improvement
- Set learning goals for next quarter

### Network Expansion
- Reach out to one new contact in AI
- Share your learnings with your team
- Contribute to a community discussion

### Project Review
- Assess progress on AI initiatives
- Document lessons learned
- Update playbooks and frameworks
